Drawing

    MDF
    • Rack Drawing: Devices should be arranged from top to bottom in the rack.
    • Ensure the number of copper ties needed to connect to the IDF is counted and documented. 
    IDF
    • Rack Drawing: Devices should be organized systematically, following the same top-to-bottom layout. 
  • Guestrooms:

    Specify requirements or configurations as needed for guestroom connectivity.

    • Phone:
      • Phone drops will be on bedside on the night stand.
      • All cables will be white.
      • Jack will be a CAT3 Keystone Jack.
    • Data:
      • Data cables will be either behind the TV or under the desk.
      • All cables will be Blue (Renovation cables will be orange).
      • Jack: CAT6 Keystone Jack.
      • Cable: CAT6 riser (unless specified by client).
    • TV:
      • TV cables will be behind the TV.
      • Cable: RG6Q.
  • Common Area:
    • Meeting Rooms:
      • Need one Ethernet connection and one HDMI port.
      • Speakers are required.
  • All Desks (GM, Sales, Work Area):
    • Four CAT6 drops are needed near each desk.
  • All Front Desks:
    • Six CAT6 drops are required within the furniture.
  • BOH (Back of the House):
    • Volume controls are needed for each zone.
    • A data cable is required for the time clock.
    • A phone jack is needed for the fax machine.
  • Pool:
    • An access point is required.
    • A pool phone is needed.
    • If necessary, speakers will be installed.
  • Business Center:
    • Four data points are required for the printer and two computers.
  • Employee Breakroom:
    • A speaker is required.
    • A phone connection is needed.
    • A camera will be installed.
  • General Rules for Access Point Placement:
    • Access points (APs) are placed in every other room unless specified otherwise.
    • Common area access points should be roughly 30 feet apart.
  • General Rules for Cameras:
    • Cameras should cover all exits and common areas within the property.
  • General Rules for Speakers:
    • Speakers should be placed in most common areas (meeting rooms, fitness room, lobby, breakfast area, vestibule, pool, common area bathrooms, elevator lobby, employee breakroom).
    • Speakers should be spaced 6 feet apart.
